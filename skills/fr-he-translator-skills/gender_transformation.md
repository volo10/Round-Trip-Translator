# Gender Transformation Skill

## Purpose
Correctly handle gender transformation between French and Hebrew, understanding that grammatical gender does NOT transfer between languages.

## Critical Rule

**NEVER assume Hebrew gender from French gender!**

French and Hebrew have independent gender systems. Each noun must be looked up independently.

## Examples of Gender Mismatch

| French | French Gender | Hebrew | Hebrew Gender |
|--------|---------------|--------|---------------|
| la plage (beach) | Feminine | החוף | **Masculine** |
| le soleil (sun) | Masculine | השמש | **Feminine** |
| la lune (moon) | Feminine | הירח | **Masculine** |
| le livre (book) | Masculine | הספר | **Masculine** |
| la table (table) | Feminine | השולחן | **Masculine** |
| le journal (newspaper) | Masculine | העיתון | **Masculine** |
| la mer (sea) | Feminine | הים | **Masculine** |

## Hebrew Gender Indicators

### Feminine Markers
Most feminine nouns end in:
- **ה-** (he): מורה (teacher-f), ילדה (girl)
- **ת-** (tav): מכונית (car), דירה apartment ends in ה
- **ית-** suffix: ישראלית (Israeli-f), עיתונאית (journalist-f)

### Masculine Markers
- No specific ending (default)
- Many end in consonants: ספר (book), שולחן (table)

### Exceptions (Learn These!)
- Words ending in ה that are masculine: לילה (night), מטה (staff)
- Foreign words: Often keep original gender or default masculine

## Agreement Chains

When translating, ensure ALL agreements match the Hebrew gender:

### French (Feminine): La belle plage est grande
### Hebrew (Masculine): החוף היפה הוא גדול

Note: "plage" is feminine in French, but "חוף" is masculine in Hebrew.
- Adjective "יפה" stays same (no gender ending change needed for this word)
- "הוא" (he/it) used, not "היא" (she/it)
- "גדול" masculine form (not "גדולה")

## Decision Process

1. **Identify French noun** and its gender
2. **DISCARD French gender** - it's irrelevant!
3. **Look up Hebrew equivalent** noun
4. **Determine Hebrew gender** from the Hebrew word itself
5. **Apply Hebrew gender** to all adjectives, verbs, pronouns

## Verb Gender Agreement

Hebrew verbs show gender in past and future:

| Tense | Masculine | Feminine |
|-------|-----------|----------|
| Past (he/she ate) | אכל | אכלה |
| Future (he/she will eat) | יאכל | תאכל |
| Present (he/she eats) | אוכל | אוכלת |

## Common Pitfalls

1. **Auto-transferring gender**: "la maison" (f) → don't assume בית is feminine (it's masculine!)
2. **Adjective disagreement**: After finding correct Hebrew gender, adjectives must match
3. **Pronoun errors**: Using "היא" for French feminine nouns that are masculine in Hebrew

## Quality Criteria
- Hebrew gender determined from Hebrew word, not French
- All adjectives agree with Hebrew noun gender
- Verbs conjugated for correct Hebrew gender
- Pronouns match Hebrew gender system
