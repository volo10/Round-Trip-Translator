# Partitive Article Usage Skill

## Purpose
Master French partitive articles (du, de la, de l', des) which have no direct English equivalent.

## The Partitive System

French requires partitive articles before nouns when expressing "some" or an unspecified quantity.

### Article Forms

| Gender | Partitive | Example |
|--------|-----------|---------|
| Masculine | du | du pain (some bread) |
| Feminine | de la | de la viande (some meat) |
| Before vowel | de l' | de l'eau (some water) |
| Plural | des | des pommes (some apples) |

## When to Use Partitives

### 1. Unspecified Quantities
English uses "some" or no article; French requires partitive.

**Examples:**
- "I eat bread" → "Je mange **du** pain"
- "She drinks water" → "Elle boit **de l'**eau"
- "We buy apples" → "Nous achetons **des** pommes"

### 2. Abstract Nouns
- "He has courage" → "Il a **du** courage"
- "She shows patience" → "Elle montre **de la** patience"

### 3. With Expressions of Quantity (EXCEPTION)
After quantity expressions, use "de" alone (no article):

- beaucoup de (a lot of)
- peu de (a little of)
- assez de (enough of)
- trop de (too much of)

**Examples:**
- "A lot of bread" → "beaucoup **de** pain" (not "du")
- "Too much water" → "trop **d'**eau"

### 4. After Negation (EXCEPTION)
Partitive becomes "de" (or "d'") after negative:

- "I don't eat bread" → "Je ne mange pas **de** pain"
- "She doesn't drink water" → "Elle ne boit pas **d'**eau"

**Exception to exception:** With être, keep the partitive:
- "It's not bread" → "Ce n'est pas **du** pain"

## Decision Process

1. Identify if noun takes partitive (uncountable/unspecified quantity)
2. Check for quantity expressions (use "de" only)
3. Check for negation (use "de"/"d'" only)
4. Determine gender and choose correct form (du/de la/de l'/des)

## Common Patterns

| English | French |
|---------|--------|
| I want coffee | Je veux **du** café |
| She needs help | Elle a besoin **d'**aide |
| We don't have money | Nous n'avons pas **d'**argent |
| A lot of people | Beaucoup **de** gens |
| Some friends | **Des** amis |

## Quality Criteria
- Correct partitive form selected
- Quantity expressions handled correctly
- Negation rules applied properly
- Gender agreement maintained
