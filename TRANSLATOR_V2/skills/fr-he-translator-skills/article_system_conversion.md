# Article System Conversion Skill

## Purpose
Convert between French's complex article system and Hebrew's simpler system.

## French Article Types

### Definite Articles (le, la, l', les)
Specific, known items

### Indefinite Articles (un, une, des)
Non-specific items

### Partitive Articles (du, de la, de l', des)
Unspecified quantities

## Hebrew Article System

Hebrew has only ONE article: **ה** (ha) - the definite article

### Key Differences

| French | Hebrew | Notes |
|--------|--------|-------|
| le/la/l'/les | ה- prefix | Definite article |
| un/une | (none) | No indefinite article! |
| du/de la/de l' | (none) | No partitive! |
| des (plural indefinite) | (none) | Just plural noun |

## Conversion Rules

### 1. Definite → ה Prefix

| French | Hebrew |
|--------|--------|
| le livre | הספר |
| la maison | הבית |
| l'homme | האיש |
| les enfants | הילדים |

**Note:** ה is a PREFIX attached to the word, not a separate word.

### 2. Indefinite → No Article

| French | Hebrew | Literal |
|--------|--------|---------|
| un livre | ספר | (book) |
| une maison | בית | (house) |
| des livres | ספרים | (books) |

Hebrew relies on context to show indefiniteness.

### 3. Partitive → No Article

| French | Hebrew | Literal |
|--------|--------|---------|
| du pain | לחם | (bread) |
| de la viande | בשר | (meat) |
| de l'eau | מים | (water) |

### 4. Preposition + Article Combinations

French combines prepositions with articles; Hebrew uses prefix stacking:

| French | Hebrew | Components |
|--------|--------|------------|
| à la maison | לבית | ל (to) + בית |
| du livre | מהספר | מ (from) + ה (the) + ספר |
| au parc | לפארק | ל (to) + פארק |
| dans le jardin | בגן | ב (in) + גן |
| avec les amis | עם החברים | עם (with) + ה + חברים |

## Special Cases

### 1. Possessives
French: mon livre (my book)
Hebrew: הספר שלי (the-book of-me) or ספרי (my-book)

### 2. Generic Statements
French often uses definite for generic; Hebrew varies:
- "J'aime le chocolat" (I like THE chocolate) → "אני אוהב שוקולד" (no article)

### 3. Abstract Nouns
French uses articles with abstract nouns; Hebrew often doesn't:
- "La vie est belle" → "החיים יפים" (life is beautiful) - Hebrew keeps definite here
- "J'ai besoin de patience" → "אני צריך סבלנות" (no article)

## Decision Process

1. Identify French article type (definite/indefinite/partitive)
2. For definite: Add ה prefix to Hebrew noun
3. For indefinite/partitive: Use bare Hebrew noun
4. Handle preposition+article combinations as prefixes
5. Check for special cases (possessives, generic, abstract)

## Quality Criteria
- Definite articles converted to ה prefix
- Indefinite and partitive articles omitted
- Preposition+article combinations handled correctly
- Natural Hebrew article usage maintained
